My First Time Programming

May 12

I always liked learning about different stuff. Programming was on the top of my list. Since I was 13, I wanted to become a hacker (in those days, I did not know what it meant to be a hacker, just what the movies showed). Not having a computer at that time made my journey of being a hacker almost impossible. After a while, I forgot about it and moved on, having that feeling inside of me. Few years later, I got accepted into a program called Access Code. The program required that students spend time practicing HyperText Markup Language (HTML). HTML is the skeleton of everython you see when you open a website: the paragraph, the titles, the images. Once we completed the online course, we participated in a workshop that taught us how to create a websites. The experiences provided me the opportunity of learning more about programming and what it enteals to be a developer. I felt as a child trying to learn a new language.
