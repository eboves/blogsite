My Programming Journey

Hi, my name is Elvis Boves, a Mechanical Engineer in Technology. I have a love and hate relationship with programming. I have been trying to become a developer for more than seven years! -Wow! now that I was remembering the first time I started programming, I started laughing, it had been seven years!! Time does fly-. I got accepted in non-profit organization called C4Q (Coalition for Queens) back in 2015. They help people with non programming background and help them to get hired as a developer. They taught us Java so that we develop Android applications. I was part of the second cohort they offered. I enjoyed the experience, and I learned a lot even though I did not kept going; also, I met some really cool people during the program. The cohort lasted six months, and during that time, we learned the basics of Java to develop. I do not know why I did not stick with it, but it was not appealing to me at the time; maybe I had impostor syndrome ,or perhabs I was not ready at the time, who knows.

After a year or two, I got a scholarship at Fullstack Academy doing the front-end developer program they have. As you may know, at this point, I was learning HTML, CSS, and Javascript. For my final project, I went, and took the deliberation of learning Angular; up to this day, I still remember the struggle I had with it, but I liked it nevertheless. And again, after the program, I did not continue programming; at that time, I felt that I did not learn enough, that I was not ready, that programming was not for me, and leave it like that.

After Fullstack I stopped programming for around two years. I completely forgot about it, but one day it hit me again the need to learn to program. This time, I decided to learn python. It might be the "easiest" programming language to learn. I really like it and enjoy writing python code; whoever, I got caught in what is called tutorial hell. I was watching tutorials for everything that has to do with python, but I was not writing code or creating projects. I even bought some Udemy courses but did not follow through. I was putting a lot of pressure on myself at the time, I felt stupid for not knowing or understanding a topic and that got me discouraged. And again I stopped programming altogether. Fast forward to two years ago, during the beginning of the pandemic, I got accepted into CUNY Tech Prep. It's a free program for CUNY students that are interested in learning Fullstack Web App Dev using Javascript or Data science using Python. I was taking classes when I got accepted, and I did not want to lose the opportunity. I was a bit overwhelmed with everything.

I took advantage of the pandemic, and that classes were online, moved to the Dominican Republic to save some money; since I was not working at the time and classes were remote. That way, I could focus on my school classes as well as the program classes. During the program, we were learning Machine Learning. We used python and learned about pandas and other python libraries. I found the program hard, maybe because of everything that was happening. I got discouraged. Feeling overwhelmed and stressed all the time got me nowhere. I was not handling the program's homework on time. I felt like I did not belong there; I felt like an outsider. Most of the students were taking college classes related to the topic. The final project was in team of two. We built a machine model that analyzes the sentiments of twitter's tweets. He did most of the heavy lifting. I did more the front-end. Do not get me wrong, I love learning, and I learned a lot during this program, but I was not ready yet. At this point you already know what I did next, yup!, you guessed it, I stopped programming completely. I told myself that I was done with programming; after all these years and programs I went through. I did want to deal with programming.

I honestly do not remember where I saw it first, but I saw the Citi group partnership with Udacity. They were offering a Full-stack Dev scholarship. At first, I did not apply since I told myself that I did not want to do the same again. I did not want to feel discouraged one more time, so I close the window and forgot about it. Call it faith if you want, but five days before the application's deadline, I saw the Citi group scholarship again. Honestly, I did want to apply for the scholarship since I was "done with programming"; however, I applied. To complete the application process, you have to complete the HTML and CSS courses Udacity offers. A week or so later, we received the final assignment, a test based on Java, SQL, HTML, and CSS. This "exam" that we had to take would determine whether or not we get granted a scholarship. They were offering only 100 scholarships, which meant that the first 100 participants with the highest score would get accepted. To my surprise, I got accepted!!!!

Now, you may say "yo Elvis, what makes this time different from the previous three you had?" and to that, I would say "his time I am building stuff" -It was not as if I was not building projects before. Now, I am practicing programming every day and building stuff on my own. Currently, I am creating my website. I can say now that I am starting to like programming. I know it will be hard. I am 100% sure that it will be challenging, but I know 1000% that in the end is going to be worth it. I know that with a goal in mind and with some discipline you can accomplish anything you set your mind to. Let's make this time count!!

Elvis Boves
